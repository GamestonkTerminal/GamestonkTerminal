usage: pos [-l LIMIT] [-s {sv,sv_pct,nsv,nsv_dollar,dpp,dpp_dollar}] [-r]

Get dark pool short positions. [Source: Stockgrid]

optional arguments:
  -l LIMIT, --limit LIMIT
                        Limit of tickers to display. (default: 10)
  -s {sv,sv_pct,nsv,nsv_dollar,dpp,dpp_dollar}, --sort {sv,sv_pct,nsv,nsv_dollar,dpp,dpp_dollar}
                        Field for which to sort by, where 'sv': Short Vol. [1M], 'sv_pct': Short Vol. %, 'nsv': Net Short Vol. [1M], 'nsv_dollar': Net Short Vol. ($100M), 'dpp': DP Position [1M], 'dpp_dollar': DP Position ($1B)
                        (default: dpp_dollar)
  -r, --reverse         Data is sorted in descending order by default. Reverse flag will sort it in an ascending way. Only works when raw data is displayed. (default: False)


Examples:
- To display the top 10 dark pool short positions: stocks/dps/pos
- To display the top 20 dark pool short positions: stocks/dps/pos -l 20
- To sort dark pool short positions by Short Vol. [1M]: stocks/dps/pos -s sv
- To sort dark pool short positions by Net Short Vol. ($100M) and display the top 15: stocks/dps/pos -l 15 -s nsv_dollar
- To display dark pool short positions sorted by DP Position [1M] in ascending order: stocks/dps/pos -s dpp -r
- To display the top 5 dark pool short positions sorted by Short Vol. % in ascending order: stocks/dps/pos -l 5 -s sv_pct -r
- To display the top 30 dark pool short positions sorted by Net Short Vol. [1M]: stocks/dps/pos -l 30 -s nsv
- To display dark pool short positions sorted by DP Position ($1B) in ascending order: stocks/dps/pos -s dpp_dollar -r
