usage: plot [-p] [-x {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}] [-y {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}] [-c {smile}]

Shows a plot for the given x and y variables

optional arguments:
  -p, --put             Shows puts instead of calls (default: False)
  -x {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}, --x_axis {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}
                        ltd- last trade date, s- strike, lp- last price, b- bid, a- ask,c- change, pc- percent change, v- volume, oi- open interest, iv- implied volatility (default: s)
  -y {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}, --y_axis {ltd,s,lp,b,a,c,pc,v,oi,iv}
                        ltd- last trade date, s- strike, lp- last price, b- bid, a- ask,c- change, pc- percent change, v- volume, oi- open interest, iv- implied volatility (default: iv)
  -c {smile}, --custom {smile}
                        Choose from already created graphs (default: None)


Examples:
- To plot implied volatility (iv) versus strike (s) for call options of a specific stock: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -x s -y iv
- To visualize the relationship between volume (v) and open interest (oi) for put options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -p -x v -y oi
- For a custom "smile" graph of a stock's options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -c smile
- To display a plot comparing the last price (lp) and bid (b) for call options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -x lp -y b
- To examine the relationship between ask (a) and percent change (pc) for put options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -p -x a -y pc
- To generate a plot of change (c) against strike (s) for a stock's options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -x s -y c
- To visualize the correlation between last trade date (ltd) and volume (v) for call options: stocks/load <SYMBOL>/options/plot -x ltd -y v
